The Unrest Collaborative.

We help social and environmental justice organizations work effectively,
mostly through technology.


We partner with organizations that
have real problems to solve,
and need to use technology to solve them.

Basin

We're partnering with Pioneer Valley Open Science and the MIT Media Lab to build open-source environmental monitoring hardware and software. The technology we build will collect data about and help identify solutions for local environmental problems.

Together API

In partnership with Uplift Alliance, we're collecting and providing data about organizations working for the public good.

The Unrest Foundation

Our microfoundation enables peer-to-peer grantmaking, allowing small groups to fund individuals with creative ideas to address social justice problems.

Who are we?

Unrest is spearheaded by Matt Cloyd, a former organizer with Code for Boston and former product manager in the public sector.

Unrest partners with organizations to help them build internal capacity. It'll be rare to see Unrest working on their own.

Why "Unrest"?

We want to remind ourselves and others that we can never rest or let our guard down when driving real change.

We think the name has just a touch of mischief, which will remind us to challenge ourselves to go outside the bounds of normal business -- perhaps, for example, helping activists with encryption and protest technology.

Want to partner with us?

We're open to working with any non-profit, government organization, B Corporation, or for-profit organization with a strong, explicit, and critical social mission.

Tell us who you are and what you need, and we'll get back to you quickly.
